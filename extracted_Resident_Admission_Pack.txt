MonitoringSwan Care Home operates Closed Circuit Television (CCTV) within communal areas only to help maintain the safety, security and wellbeing of residents, visitors, staff and the premises. CCTV is not installed within residents' bedrooms, bathrooms, toilets or any other area where a person would reasonably expect complete privacy.The purpose of CCTV is to:Promote the safety and wellbeing of residents, staff and visitors.Assist in the prevention and detection of crime, abuse, neglect, violence or anti-social behaviour.Support safeguarding investigations.Protect the Home's property and assets.Assist with investigations into accidents, incidents or complaints where appropriate.CCTV recordings are normally retained for 7 days, after which they are automatically overwritten unless they are required for safeguarding investigations, police enquiries, insurance claims, legal proceedings or regulatory investigations. Where recordings are retained for these purposes, they will only be kept for as long as necessary.Access to CCTV RecordingsAccess to CCTV recordings is strictly controlled and limited to the Nominated Individual.If the Nominated Individual is unavailable, access may only be authorised to another Senior Manager or Director acting on behalf of the Nominated Individual where there is a legitimate operational, safeguarding, legal or regulatory reason. Any access must be authorised, proportionate and recorded within the Home's CCTV Access Log.CCTV recordings will not be viewed routinely and will only be accessed where there is a genuine business, safeguarding or legal requirement.Confidentiality and Information SharingSwan Care Home is committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring that all personal information is handled confidentially and securely.Your personal information will only be accessed by authorised members of staff who require the information to provide your care, fulfil their professional responsibilities or carry out their role safely and effectively.Information will only be shared where it is necessary and lawful to do so. This may include sharing information with:Your General Practitioner (GP).Hospital staff.District Nurses.Community Nurses.Community Mental Health Teams.Pharmacists.Allied Healthcare Professionals involved in your care.Ambulance Services.The Local Authority.The Care Quality Commission (CQC).Safeguarding Adults Teams.The Police.Coroners.Emergency Services.Other statutory or regulatory bodies where required by law.Information will not be shared with family members, friends or any other third party without your consent, unless:You have authorised us to do so.Your appointed Health and Welfare Lasting Power of Attorney or Court Appointed Deputy has legal authority to act on your behalf.There is a safeguarding concern.Sharing is necessary to protect you or another person from serious harm.We are required to do so by law or by a regulatory authority.Data ProtectionAll personal information is processed in accordance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR), the Data Protection Act 2018, the Mental Capacity Act 2005, and Swan Care Home's Confidentiality, Information Governance and Data Protection Policies.Residents and their representatives have the right to:Understand how their information is used.Request access to their personal information.Request corrections to inaccurate information.Withdraw consent where appropriate.Raise concerns regarding the handling of their information.Make a complaint to the Home or to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) if they believe their information has not been handled correctly.Resident / Representative ConfirmationI confirm that the above information regarding CCTV, confidentiality, information sharing and data protection has been explained to me in a way that I understand.
